About Galway

Galway is a modern, thriving, bohemian city located on the west coast of Ireland. The city was first settled in in the year 1124 AD. Modern Galway is a joy to explore with its labyrinthine cobbled streets, colorful shop facades and busy café/ bar culture. There is a pack calendar of festivals and events all year round. Old Ireland has not been lost though, you will still find lots of open fires, traditional music, and open and friendly people ready to welcome you with a smile. Round off your afternoon by taking a stroll along the promenade and watch the sunset over Galway Bay.

Getting Here

Unfortunately Galway doesn't have an international airport within the city limits. However there are 2 airports that serve the city. Shannon international airport and Knock airport. Both airports are a 1 hour drive from Galway but Shannon is an international airport and the 3rd busiest airport in the country, so you will have a much better range of flights to choose from if you fly into Shannon. Our Top Recommendations

The Quays Bar

The Quays Bar can be found in Galway’s famous Latin quarter. A traditional Irish pub the original structure is over 400 years old. The bar has live music every night of the week and also serves food all day. A great place for a cosy pint or a lively evening.

Cliffs Of Moher

Only a 90 minute drive from Galway the Cliffs of Moher are a spectacle you have to see if you're in Galway. They rise to 702 feet (214 m) at their highest point and range for 8 kms (5 miles) over the Atlantic ocean. The scale of the cliffs is breath taking.

Esquires Coffee

Esquires Coffee is all about exceptional coffee in a comfortable, warm and friendly environment. Located right in the heart of Galway just off Eyre Square this coffee shop is a great place to kick back, relax, enjoy a cup of coffee and watch the world go by.

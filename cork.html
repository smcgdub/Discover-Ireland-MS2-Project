About Dublin

Dublin, the capital of Ireland and one of Europe’s most popular city break destinations. Home to over 1 million people Dublin is a young vibrant city and has a pack calendar full of events all year long. The city has a mild climate which means it is suitable to visit at any time of the year. Whether you’re here for business or pleasure, you are guaranteed to have a fantastic time.

Getting Around

The Luas is Dublin’s high speed light rail network. The Luas is the quickest and easiest ways to navigate your way around the city. There are two Luas lines in Dublin, the red line and the green line. The red line starts at the 3 arena in the east of the city and runs in a westerly direction. It passes the other major transport hubs in the city such as Connolly train station, Busaras (national bus terminal) and Heuston train station. The green line runs from the northside of the city, all the way through the city centre, and then all they way to the suburbs of the southside of the city.

The DART (Dublin Area Rapid Transit) is an overground train that runs along the east coast of the greater Dublin area. It starts at the beautiful harbour town of Malahide and runs down the east coast of the country as far as Greystones in county Wicklow. The DART is a great way to get out of the city centre and take a relaxing, scenic journey to discover some of the beautiful seaside towns along Ireland’s ancient east coast.

Dublin has an extensive bus network that operates across the entire city. The bus offers a quick and cheap way to get around the city. Dublin bus have an app available on both IOS and Android. Every Dublin bus stop has a unique number, you can usually find it on top of the bus stop. You can use this number with the app to get real time passenger information about your journey.

Dublin operates a public bike rental scheme and its similar to bike schemes operated in other countries. Simply sign up online, provide all of your details and you can then use any bike available from a docking station.

Free Now is Ireland’s leading taxi app. While the Uber app does work in Ireland, only licensed taxi drivers are able to provide taxi rids by law. Not a lot of taxi drivers use the Uber app so if you're going to need a taxi Free Now is the app to use.
